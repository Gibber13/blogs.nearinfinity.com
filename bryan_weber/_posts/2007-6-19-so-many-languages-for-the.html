--- permalink: /blogs/bryan_weber/so_many_languages_for_the.html layout: blogs title: So many languages for the JVM... date: 2007-06-19 21:14:49 -04:00 tags: Java --- {% raw %} Java, Jython, BeanShell, JRuby, Groovy, Scala... with so many languages available for the JVM now, how do I know which one is right for me? Language selection is becoming a design consideration that architects have to take into consideration. Language selection is becoming part of the answer to the question "How am I going to accomplish...?" When thinking about how to accomplish something, it often helps to think about "What am I trying to accomplish?" and "Why am I trying to accomplish it?" Some things to consider:
  • Strongly Typed?
  • Does implementation lag another language/specification?
  • Object Oriented/Functional/Procedural/etc?
  • Level of support required?
  • Level of expertise available?
  • Life expectancy?
  • Language features (Closures, native Regex support, etc) required/desired?
  • Performance requirements?
  • Ease of interaction with other JVM languages/libraries?
  • Re-usability by other JVM languages/libraries?
  • Ease of use?
  • Ease of readability?
  • What tools/support/debugging options are available?
  • Should I wait for invokedynamic byte code instruction?
